During game hours, the system checks live scores every 10 minutes. When a team has a 20+ point lead at halftime, it triggers an alert for potential "garbage time" regression - where the losing team often outscores in the second half as the winning team rests starters.

The Theory

Garbage Time Regression: When a team has a commanding lead (20+ points), coaches often rest their star players in the second half. Meanwhile, the losing team plays their bench players more aggressively to gain experience and maintain competitiveness.

This creates a statistical opportunity: the losing team often "covers the spread" in the second half even though they lose the game. Live betting on the trailing team's second-half performance can be profitable.
